Breathtaking Destinations in Asia

Top 3 Destinations

China

Visit China if you have a keen interest Chinese culture and Skyscrapers

Malaysia

Visit Malaysia if you love Malaysian food and want to be on top of the Petronas Twin Towers

Singapore

Visit Singapore if you love Singaporean food and want to see the Marina Bay Sands

China's Tourist Attractions

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China, one of China's most ancient history. It is said to be the longest construction made by mere humans.

Forbidden City

The Forbidden City is a complex palace located in Central Beijing, home to the emperors of the Ming Dynasty and Qing Dynasty

The Bund

The Bund is a riverside area located in Central Shanghai, on the west bank of Huangpu River. It can also be known as the "Museum of Buildings".

Malaysia's Tourist Attractions

Petronas Twin Towers

The Petronas Twin Towers are the twin skyscapers located in Kuala Lumpur. They were said to be the tallest buildings from 1998 to 2004, until Taipei 101 took their place.

Batu Caves

Batu Caves, a limestone that consists of many temples and caves, located in Selangor. Batu Caves is known to be one of the most famous Hindu Shrines outside of India.

Menara Kuala Tower

Menara Kuala Lumpur, also known as the Kuala Lumpur Tower, is a significant Landmark that in Malaysia as it is know to be the tallest Telecommunications Tower in South East Asia.

Singapore's Tourist Attractions

Marina Bay Sands

Marina Bay Sands, a fascinating integrated resort located in Singapore. It comprises of luxury shopping centres, five star hotels, and fancy restaurants.

Gardens by the Bay

Gardens by the Bay is a nature park located in Central Singapore. It conssits of three unique waterfront gardens: Bay South, Bay Central, and Bay East.

Singapore Flyer

Singapore Flyer, the largest Giant Observation Wheel, is known to be Asia's most biggest tourist attraction. The height of the Flyer comes up to 165m.
